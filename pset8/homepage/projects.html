Masters Thesis: Capturing the effects of variation on Turbine Blade performance

Something I always wondered when looking at degraded engine components at GE Aviation was the effect that this degradation had on the performance of the engine on wing, particularly in the critical turbine section. I took the opportunity to study this for my masters thesis, which you can read here.

I used MATLAB to devise a model that could randomly perturb a set of airfoil coordinates in a way which closely replicates the variations present in a newly manufactured or degraded set of blades. I then ran CFD simulations on these perturbed blade coordinate sets using MISES and analysed the results using MATLAB and Python.

Unmanned Aircraft System: Design, Build, Fly

In my 3rd year of university, I worked as part of a team of 5 to design and construct an Unmanned Aircraft System according to stringent IMechE requirements, with the objective of providing humanitarian aid to disaster locations. I acted as chief propulsion engineer and procurement lead. This mix of roles allowed me to build my technical skills while gaining project management experience of managing budget constraints and part lead times.

Our aircraft utilised a carbon fibre fuselage frame with 3D printed joints and a lightweight design aimed at doing multiple rapid journeys.
